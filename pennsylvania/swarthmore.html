Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, reputable roofing contractors must be properly registered and insured before work begins. For example, Pennsylvania requires Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) to be registered through the PA Attorney General’s Office (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). Before you sign in Swarthmore (or anywhere in Delaware County), verify your contractor’s HIC number and that it matches the company address shown on the agreement.

Permits are municipality-specific. In many Pennsylvania municipalities, a building permit is required for full roof replacements (especially tear-offs), while some like-for-like shingle replacements may be exempt—however, older Swarthmore-area homes often trigger additional inspection requirements once flashing, ventilation, or deck repairs are involved. The key is to confirm with the local building department for reroofing thresholds and tear-off/inspection rules before work starts.

Ice and water shield requirements also matter: Pennsylvania residential code based on the IRC expects ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with a history of ice dam formation (which applies broadly statewide). Ask your contractor to confirm the required minimum installation height on your roof.

For storm-related damage, document everything before cleanup. Request a written scope of the contractor’s findings to support your insurance claim. Pennsylvania law also prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible—anything that suggests “no out-of-pocket” should be treated as a red flag. Finally, verify workers’ compensation coverage using the WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database) and require proof of coverage; uninsured roofers create liability exposure for you as the homeowner.

Step-by-step homeowner checks in Swarthmore: (1) verify the HIC registration online, (2) request a current certificate of insurance (general liability and workers’ comp), (3) confirm workers’ comp status via WCAIS, (4) ask whether a permit is required for your exact scope and get the permit plan in writing, and (5) review the proposed scope for ice-and-water shield and flashing/ventilation details.

When decking is compromised, replacing sooner is usually the safest long-term financial decision.

How to choose a roofing contractor in Swarthmore

Choosing the right roofing contractor in Swarthmore is mostly about verifying credentials and matching the quoted scope to what your roof actually needs. Start by checking Pennsylvania licensing and registration: verify the contractor’s Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) information through the PA Attorney General’s office website (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). Then confirm workers’ compensation coverage in the WCAIS system. Finally, ask for a current certificate of insurance—make sure the certificate names the correct legal business and shows coverage appropriate for roofing work (not just general liability).

Next, collect 2–3 itemized written quotes. You want a breakdown that separates labor from materials, lists underlayment/ice-and-water coverage, shows what will be replaced versus repaired, and explains disposal and site protection. A “lump sum” quote without scope detail is hard to compare. If you’re dealing with storm damage, ask whether the estimate includes storm-related documentation you can use with your insurer.

Warranty matters in two layers: workmanship and materials. Confirm the workmanship warranty length, who backs it, and whether it’s transferable. Also ask about the manufacturer warranty and what installation requirements must be met for it to remain valid—this is where correct flashing, ventilation, and ice-and-water installation can be critical.

Payment schedule should be conservative. Avoid paying more than about 10–15% upfront. Use a holdback until the job is complete and debris removal and cleanup are verified. Ask for a written arrival estimate and an expected completion window, especially because Delaware County crews often get booked faster in peak season for repairs than full replacements.
