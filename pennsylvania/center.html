A homeowner protection step-by-step: (1) check HIC registration online; (2) request COI (general liability) and confirm it matches the HIC/name; (3) verify workers’ comp in WCAIS; (4) confirm whether your permit is required and who pulls it.

What causes ice dams on roofs in Center?

Ice dams form when heat escapes from your home and melts snow on the roof. The meltwater then refreezes at the colder edges—often at the eaves—creating a ridge of ice that blocks normal drainage. In Center and throughout Beaver County, ice dams are especially common on older roofs because insulation and ventilation details may be outdated, and because freeze-thaw cycles swing repeatedly through the winter.

Common causes include insufficient attic insulation, poor soffit-to-ridge ventilation, air leakage from the living space into the attic, and roof systems that don’t include adequate ice-and-water shield at the eaves. A third factor is roof geometry: valleys, dormers, and complex gables can encourage uneven warming and refreezing. If your home was built before 1980 (63.1% of Center’s stock, U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2024), the ventilation and insulation setup may not match today’s performance expectations.

When you’re evaluating repairs or replacement, ask your contractor how they’ll reduce ice dam risk. That usually includes correct ice-and-water shield installation height at the eaves, improving ventilation, and sealing airflow pathways.
