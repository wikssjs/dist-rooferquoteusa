Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, roofing contractors are expected to meet licensing and job-start requirements before they ever touch your roof. First, verify that your contractor is registered as a Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office at hic.attorneygeneral.gov. Ask for the HIC number and confirm it online before signing. For roofing, full roof replacements typically require a building permit, though rules can vary by municipality in and around Hermitage. Many townships require permits for complete replacements (not repairs), while some boroughs may exempt like-for-like shingle swaps—so confirm with Hermitage’s code office or your local permit desk before work begins.

Ice and water shield is another common compliance item. Pennsylvania residential code (based on the IRC) generally requires ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with a history of ice dam formation—covering essentially all of Pennsylvania. During your estimate, ask the contractor to confirm the required minimum installation height at the eaves and how they’ll detail transitions at valleys, chimneys, and penetrations.

For storm insurance claims, document before cleanup: photos, measurements, and a written scope of loss. Request a written scope from the contractor that you can attach to your carrier submission. Pennsylvania law also prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible; that can be treated as insurance fraud.

Finally, protect yourself with Workers’ Compensation verification. You can confirm coverage through WCAIS (PA’s workers’ comp database). If the crew isn’t insured, you may assume liability exposure. A simple homeowner workflow in Hermitage: (1) check HIC at hic.attorneygeneral.gov, (2) request certificate of insurance (general liability) and verify dates, (3) check workers’ comp via WCAIS, (4) confirm permit requirement with the municipal office for your address, and (5) ensure the estimate includes the ice-barrier detail and warranty terms in writing.

How to choose a roofing contractor in Hermitage

Choosing the right roofing contractor in Hermitage isn’t about finding the lowest number—it’s about hiring someone who can prove credentials, explain the scope clearly, and stand behind workmanship in a market where older homes (U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2024) often hide decking and ventilation issues after tear-off. Start with licensing and certification checks: Pennsylvania requires HIC registration for home improvement work through the PA Attorney General’s Office (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). Also ask whether the crew has relevant manufacturer installation training (many systems require specific installation methods to keep warranties valid). If they reference certifications, ask for the IICRC/other credential number and verify it via the issuing organization’s registry when available.

Then get 2–3 itemized written quotes. You want labor and materials broken out (shingles/membrane, underlayment/ice barrier, flashing/drip edge, ventilation items, tear-off, disposal, decking repair allowance). Avoid “lump sum only” proposals. Read the scope for exclusions: what’s not included (wood replacement limits, chimney work, gutter replacement, disposal caps, or electrical/heat cable coordination). For insurance jobs, confirm whether the contractor will include storm documentation and a written scope that matches the carrier process.

Warranty matters in Pennsylvania winters. Ask for the length of workmanship coverage and whether it’s transferable. Also ask what equipment/material warranties apply and what could void them (for example, missing ventilation requirements). Payment scheduling should protect you: never pay more than 10–15% upfront, and hold back until the job is complete and cleanup is verified. Finally, request an arrival estimate and completion window in writing so you’re not left guessing during weather delays.
