Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, roofing contractors are typically required to register as Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office. Before you sign anything, verify your contractor’s HIC number at hic.attorneygeneral.gov—that’s your quick check that the company is operating legally and can be held accountable.

Building permit rules for roof work vary by municipality. Most townships require a building permit for full roof replacements (not just minor repairs). Some boroughs may exempt “like-for-like” shingle swaps, but for Upper Merion homeowners, the safe move is to confirm with the township code office before the crew arrives—especially if you’re changing roof materials, adding ventilation changes, or replacing decking.

On the installation side, Pennsylvania residential code (based on the IRC) requires ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with a history of ice-dam formation—which effectively applies statewide. Ask your contractor to confirm the minimum installation height and where the ice-and-water shield will run.

Storm claims also require careful paperwork. Document damage before cleanup, and request a written scope of loss from the contractor so you can submit it with your insurance claim. PA law also prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible; attempts to do so can create serious issues for you.

Finally, verify workers’ compensation coverage through the PA WCAIS system (PA workers’ comp database). In Upper Merion, an uninsured crew is a liability risk for the homeowner—so ask for a certificate of insurance (COI) and proof of workers’ comp before work starts. For homeowners, the verification flow is simple: check HIC online, review the COI for current dates, confirm WC via WCAIS, and keep copies with your contract.

How to choose a roofing contractor in Upper Merion

Choosing the right contractor matters as much as the shingle or membrane you select. Start by verifying Pennsylvania licensing/registration: in addition to the contractor’s standard credentials, confirm the company is registered as a Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office at hic.attorneygeneral.gov. For workers’ compensation, you can verify coverage through PA’s workers’ comp system (WCAIS). These checks help protect you if anything goes wrong on an Upper Merion jobsite.

Then get 2–3 itemized written quotes. Ask for a breakdown that clearly lists labor, tear-off, disposal, underlayment/ice-and-water shield, flashing/drip edges, ventilation changes, decking repairs, and the warranty terms. Avoid “lump sum only” bids; roofing projects in Montgomery County commonly include unknowns discovered during tear-off, so a good contractor will explain allowances and how they handle extras.

Read the scope carefully. Does the proposal include permit handling and final inspection support? Is insurance claim documentation included, if applicable? Does it specify what’s excluded—like replacing damaged sheathing beyond a certain amount or removing attic insulation that’s needed for ventilation work?

Warranty matters too: confirm the workmanship warranty length, and distinguish it from manufacturer coverage for the materials. For payment, never pay more than about 10–15% upfront; hold back remaining payment until the job is complete and cleaned up. Finally, require a written timeline: an arrival estimate, the expected start date, and a completion window.
