Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, roofing contractors are expected to be registered as Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office. Before you sign anything, verify your contractor’s HIC number on the registry at hic.attorneygeneral.gov. This is one of the simplest steps Warwick homeowners can take to reduce risk.

Permits for roof work vary by municipality. In most Pennsylvania townships, you typically need a building permit for full roof replacements, while like-for-like repairs may be treated differently. Some boroughs can exempt certain in-kind shingle replacements, but you should confirm with the local code office before work begins. If the project includes structural changes—like redecking beyond minor spots—or switching roof systems, expect permits and inspections to be more likely.

Two specific compliance topics matter year-round:

  • Ice and water shield: Residential code based on the IRC generally requires ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with a history of ice dam formation, which applies broadly across Pennsylvania. Ask your contractor what minimum installation height they will meet.
  • Storm claims and deductible: Document damage thoroughly before cleanup and request a written scope of loss for your insurance submission. Pennsylvania law prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible (it can be treated as insurance fraud).

To verify credentials step-by-step in Warwick: check the contractor’s HIC online, request an up-to-date certificate of insurance for general liability, and verify workers’ compensation using the WCAIS database. Then confirm those policies match the company name on your contract and the job address.

By contrast, a full replacement on an older roof can land closer to the $18,000–$20,000 band depending on scope, while avoiding the cycle of recurring moisture problems.

Warwick-specific housing realities also matter: older colonials and capes often have complex flashing and multiple roof planes. Those details make repairs possible, but they also mean the fastest path to a durable fix is often replacement when the roof is well past its expected life.

What is the best roofing material for Warwick's climate?

There isn’t one material that’s “best” for every Warwick home, but Pennsylvania’s winter conditions and storm exposure help narrow the decision.
