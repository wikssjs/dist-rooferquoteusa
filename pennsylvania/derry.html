Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

Ice and water shield protection matters statewide. Based on the IRC adopted in PA, residential code requires ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with a history of ice dam formation—which applies broadly across Pennsylvania. Ask your contractor to confirm the required minimum installation height and how they’ll transition it to proper flashing.

For storm insurance claims, document before cleanup. Request a written scope of loss (photos, measured damage areas, and proposed repairs) to support your claim. Also verify workers’ compensation: check WCAIS (PA’s workers’ comp database) so you’re not carrying liability if a roofer is injured on your property. Finally, keep a copy of the certificate of insurance and match the policy holder to the contractor name on your contract.

How to choose a roofing contractor in Derry

Picking a roofing contractor in Derry should start with verification. For licensing, confirm the company’s Pennsylvania Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration through the PA Attorney General’s registry (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). For workers’ comp and compliance checks, verify coverage using WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database) so you know the crew is insured for on-the-job injuries. You should also request proof of liability insurance and match the certificates to the legal name of the contractor on your estimate.

Next, get 2–3 itemized written quotes. The best quotes break down labor and materials (tear-off and disposal, underlayment/ice and water shield, deck repairs, flashing, vents, drip edge, and warranty details), rather than only providing a single lump-sum number. Read the scope carefully: confirm what’s included (permits if required, disposal, flashing around chimneys/valleys, gutter replacement if warranted, and ventilation upgrades). If you’re filing an insurance claim, ask how the contractor will document measured damage and provide photos and a written scope to match the claim.

Warranty matters too—both workmanship and manufacturer coverage should be clearly stated, including duration and any exclusions related to ventilation, installation standards, or maintenance. For payment, keep it controlled: never pay more than 10–15% upfront, and plan a holdback until the roof is complete and cleaned. Finally, schedule a clear timeline: request an arrival estimate and completion window in writing so weather delays and inspection checkpoints don’t surprise you.

What causes ice dams on roofs in Derry?

Ice dams form when heat escapes into the attic roof space and melts snow on the upper roof. That meltwater then refreezes at the colder eaves, creating a dam that forces water under shingles. In Derry and across Pennsylvania, freeze-thaw cycles and occasional ice storms make this especially noticeable, and older homes with less modern insulation, older ventilation setups, or shallow-vented roof lines are more vulnerable.

Common causes we investigate include poor attic insulation coverage, insufficient soffit-to-ridge ventilation, air leakage from the house into the attic, and missing or ineffective ice and water shield at eaves. Pennsylvania code (based on the IRC) generally expects ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with ice dam formation history, so it’s important that your contractor details the minimum installation height and how it’s applied at drip edges and transitions.

If you’re seeing persistent edge leaks or staining in winter, don’t wait for spring. Emergency tarping and temporary sealing can stop interior damage while we schedule full correction—often within the broader asphalt replacement price expectations, frequently tied to whether your roof also needs ice shield and ventilation upgrades.
