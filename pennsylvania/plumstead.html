Ice and water shield matters across Pennsylvania. Finally, verify workers’ compensation coverage using WCAIS (PA’s workers’ comp database) and ask for proof of insurance (certificate of insurance) before work starts.

Step-by-step for Plumstead homeowners: (1) check HIC status online, (2) request the certificate of insurance and confirm coverage aligns with your address/scope, (3) verify workers’ comp via WCAIS, (4) confirm whether a permit is required and who will file it, and (5) ensure the contract includes an inspected, code-compliant underlayment and flashing plan.

What causes ice dams on roofs in Plumstead?

Ice dams happen when warm air from the home melts snow on the roof, and that water refreezes near the colder roof edge/eave, forming a barrier. In Plumstead, the freeze-thaw cycle makes this pattern common. The primary causes are usually attic heat loss or poor air sealing, inadequate soffit intake and roof exhaust ventilation, and roof edge details that don’t include enough ice-barrier protection where code expects it.

On older homes built before 1980 (about 27.6% of Plumstead’s housing stock), ice dam risk is often higher because ventilation layouts and insulation levels were frequently upgraded piecemeal over time. Shallow overhangs and rooflines with valleys also concentrate cold spots where meltwater backs up. That’s why good inspections focus on attic temperature/air movement, not just the visible ice at the edge.

Local prevention should include properly installed ice-and-water shield at the eaves and valleys (with the installation height confirmed for your roof conditions), along with ventilation balance and attic air sealing. If you’re planning a replacement budget, ice-and-water shield work can move a project within the typical ranges—often discussed alongside the asphalt bands (like $9,000–$22,000 for many replacement scopes).
