Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, roofing isn’t a “do it and hope” category—there are credentials and rules homeowners should verify before work starts. Roofing contractors are typically required to register as Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). Always verify the contractor’s HIC number before signing, and make sure the name on the contract matches the registered business.

Building permits for roofing are handled at the municipal level. Most townships require a permit for full roof replacements (not repairs). Some boroughs may exempt like-for-like shingle replacement, but you should confirm with Hempfield Township or your specific municipal code office before the first tear-off. Don’t assume—permits affect scheduling and inspection paperwork.

Ice and water shield requirements are tied to the IRC-based residential code adopted in PA, and they generally apply across the Commonwealth due to ice dam formation risk. Ask your contractor to confirm the minimum installation height/coverage and how they’ll document that protection at eaves, valleys, and other vulnerable lines.

For storm insurance claims, document everything before cleanup, and request a written scope of loss to support your claim submission. Pennsylvania law also restricts contractors from waiving your deductible—waiver arrangements can fall into insurance fraud.

Workers’ comp matters for your liability exposure. Verify coverage via WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database) and request a certificate of insurance showing workers’ comp (and general liability). Step-by-step, the homeowner should: (1) check the HIC registry, (2) review the COI certificate for matching business name, (3) confirm workers’ comp coverage in WCAIS, and (4) confirm permit requirements with your municipality before the crew arrives.

How to choose a roofing contractor in Hempfield

Choosing a roofer in Hempfield should start with verifying credentials before you discuss price. First, check Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration at the PA Attorney General’s Office website (hic.attorneygeneral.gov). Next, confirm that the company’s licensing/registration information matches the name that will appear on your contract and invoice. For additional protection, look up workers’ comp coverage through WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database), and request a current certificate of insurance for general liability and workers’ comp before the first day on site.

Then get 2–3 itemized written quotes. “Itemized” should mean labor and materials breakdowns, not just a single lump sum. Make sure the quote lists tear-off scope (including disposal and dump fees), underlayment/ice and water shield coverage, flashing details, ventilation work if needed, and any decking replacement allowance. If it’s an insurance claim, ask whether the contractor includes a written scope of loss that matches what the insurer expects.

Warranty terms matter. Ask for both a workmanship warranty (how long they stand behind installation) and the manufacturer material warranty (what covers shingles or membrane failures). Don’t rely on verbal promises.

Payment schedule: never pay more than about 10–15% upfront. You should also plan a holdback until the job is complete and cleaned, with final inspection or punch list items done. Finally, timeline should be in writing: the expected arrival date and the estimated completion window (weather-dependent work should still be scheduled with clear milestones).
