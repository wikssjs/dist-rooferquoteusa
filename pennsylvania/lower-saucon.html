What causes ice dams on roofs in Lower Saucon?

Ice dams form when roof heat melts snow, and that meltwater refreezes at the eaves where temperatures are lower. In Lower Saucon and across Pennsylvania, the freeze-thaw pattern—warmer daytime temperatures followed by sub-freezing nights—creates the repeated melt/refreeze cycle that can push water under shingles. A second common cause is uneven attic temperatures from inadequate insulation or ventilation, which lets the roof surface warm unevenly. Older homes (about 53.8% built before 1980, per ACS 2024) frequently have ventilation and insulation setups that don’t match today’s performance expectations, making eaves more vulnerable.

Ice dams also get worse when underlayment at the eaves isn’t installed correctly or is worn out. Contractors typically address this by installing ice-and-water shield at required locations and making sure drip edge, ventilation intake/exhaust, and air sealing are properly tuned. If you’re seeing recurring staining in ceilings near the perimeter, it’s often more cost-effective to correct the cause during a replacement rather than repeatedly paying for leak repairs. For isolated damage you may see smaller repair pricing, but persistent ice-dam leaks commonly push projects toward a full reroof or a combination repair plus replacement plan.
