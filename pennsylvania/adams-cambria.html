Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, roofing contractors generally must be registered as Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) with the PA Attorney General’s Office at hic.attorneygeneral.gov. Before signing a contract for a roof replacement in Adams, verify the contractor’s HIC number—this is one of the quickest checks for whether you’re working with a legitimate, accountable business.

Permits vary by municipality. Most townships require a building permit for full roof replacements (not repairs). Some boroughs may exempt like-for-like shingle replacements, but you should not assume—confirm directly with Adams’ local code office before work starts. Ice-and-water shield is also a regulatory reality in Pennsylvania residential work: residential code (based on IRC) requires ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas with ice-dam history, which applies across virtually all of Pennsylvania. Ask your contractor to confirm the required minimum installation height.

For storm insurance claims, document everything before cleanup. Request a written scope of loss from the contractor to support your claim. Pennsylvania law also prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible—attempting that can create serious legal and insurance complications. Finally, confirm workers’ compensation coverage by checking via WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database) that the crew is covered; uninsured roofers create liability exposure for the homeowner.

  • Request a current certificate of insurance (general liability) and read the effective dates.
