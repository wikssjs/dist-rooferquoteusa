Next, get 2–3 itemized written quotes. “Itemized” means you should see labor and materials breakdowns, including tear-off scope, underlayment/ice barrier, ventilation work, flashing details, and decking allowance terms. What causes ice dams on roofs in Palmer?

Ice dams happen when heat escapes through the roof and melts snow on the upper portion, then the meltwater refreezes near the colder eaves where temperatures drop. In Palmer and throughout Pennsylvania, freeze-thaw cycles and winter winds make this a recurring issue—especially on older homes where ventilation and insulation can be uneven. When the roof system allows warm air into attics or the insulation isn’t properly balanced, the snowmelt process gets started early, and backup water can push under shingles even if the visible surface looks intact.

Common contributors in Palmer: inadequate soffit intake, blocked or undersized exhaust vents, air leaks from the living space into the attic, and roof geometry with valleys, dormers, or low-slope sections where water has a longer path. Homes built before 1980 make this more likely because many were installed before today’s ventilation and air-sealing best practices became common. With about 49.1% of homes built pre-1980 (U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2024), it’s normal to see more ice-dam history across neighborhood roof lines.

Good prevention includes proper insulation, air-sealing, and ice-and-water barrier installation at eaves. Ask your roofer to confirm the required ice barrier installation height per PA code practices and to show how they’ll integrate it around gutters, drip edge, and flashing details.
