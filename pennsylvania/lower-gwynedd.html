What causes ice dams on roofs in Lower Gwynedd?

Ice dams form when heat escapes through the attic and warms the snow on the roof edge, causing meltwater to run downward. When that meltwater reaches the colder eaves and gutters, it refreezes, building a ridge of ice that backs up water under shingles. In Lower Gwynedd and across Pennsylvania, freeze-thaw cycles make this problem more likely because roof temperatures swing between thaw and freeze during winter storms. Homes built before 1980 are more likely to have insulation and ventilation setups that let warm air leak into attics, and that drives melt. North-facing slopes, poorly sealed penetrations, and complex rooflines (valleys, multi-facet geometry) also increase the chance of dams because they cool more quickly and retain snow. The fix usually isn’t just “add shingles”—it’s a system: proper insulation air sealing, balanced ventilation, and correctly installed ice-and-water shield at the eaves. A good quote will specify ice-and-water protection locations and how far it’s applied, and it may include deck/underlayment replacement when needed to restore the roof edge protection.
