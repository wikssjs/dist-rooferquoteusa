Then get 2–3 itemized written quotes. You want a breakdown that separates labor and materials (tear-off, disposal, underlayment/ice-and-water, decking replacement, flashing, ventilation upgrades, and gutters if included). Warranty and payment schedule matter. Ask for: the workmanship warranty length, and how long materials are covered. For payment, never agree to more than 10–15% upfront; hold back enough until the job is complete and inspected. Finally, insist on a written timeline: arrival estimate, roof start date, and completion window.

What causes ice dams on roofs in Westtown?

Ice dams happen when heat escapes into the attic space and warms the underside of the roof sheathing. That warmth melts snow near the top of the roof, and then the meltwater refreezes at the colder eaves where temperatures drop. The result is a dam of ice that can push water under shingles—especially if ice-and-water protection isn’t installed properly or if ventilation/air sealing is inadequate.

In Westtown and across Pennsylvania, freeze-thaw cycles intensify this risk. Older roof lines (and homes built before 1980—47.1% of dwellings, U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 2024) may have older insulation layouts, gaps in air sealing, or ventilation that doesn’t move moisture and heat effectively. Roof pitch and how the roof meets gutters also matter: valleys, dormers, and long unbroken eaves can collect meltwater. That’s why Pennsylvania residential code guidance (based on IRC principles) emphasizes ice-barrier protection at eaves in areas where ice dam formation is common.

A good contractor will check attic airflow and air sealing, not just add an ice barrier. If you’re getting quotes, look for confirmation of ice-and-water shield coverage at the correct eave and valley areas and ask how they’ll handle the insulation and ventilation setup.
