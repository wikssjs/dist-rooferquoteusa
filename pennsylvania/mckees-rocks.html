Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

For McKees Rocks homeowners, doing things the right way starts with hiring a contractor who is properly registered and insured in Pennsylvania. Roofing contractors are required to register as Home Improvement Contractors (HIC) through the PA Attorney General’s Office at hic.attorneygeneral.gov. Before you sign, verify the contractor’s HIC number matches the company doing the work—don’t rely on a business card.

Next, understand permits. Building permit requirements for roofing replacements vary by municipality in Pennsylvania. Most townships and many boroughs require a building permit for full roof replacements (new roofing scope), while some “like-for-like” shingle swaps may be exempt in certain borough scenarios. Confirm with the local code office in the specific McKees Rocks area rules apply to your address before work begins.

Ice-and-water shield protection is also important. Pennsylvania residential code (based on IRC standards) requires ice-barrier protection at the eaves in areas with a history of ice dam formation—this effectively applies across much of Pennsylvania. Ask your contractor to confirm the required minimum installation height.

For storm insurance claims: document everything before cleanup and request a written scope of loss from your contractor to support the claim. Pennsylvania law also prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible—attempts to do so can raise fraud concerns. Finally, verify workers’ comp coverage through WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database). You’ll also want a certificate of insurance showing general liability. A homeowner’s liability exposure increases when crews are uninsured.

Step-by-step for McKees Rocks: (1) check HIC at the Attorney General registry; (2) request written proof of general liability and workers’ comp; (3) verify workers’ comp via WCAIS; (4) confirm whether a permit is needed for a full replacement with the local building/codes office; and (5) keep copies of your contract, permit paperwork (if applicable), and all insurance-related documentation.
